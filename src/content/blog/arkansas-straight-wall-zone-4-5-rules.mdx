**Yes—in specific zones.** Arkansas Deer Zones 4 and 5, the state's traditional shotgun-and-muzzleloader-only regions, now allow non-semi-automatic rifles chambered for straight-wall centerfire cartridges .30 caliber or larger during modern gun season. This regulation, adopted by the Arkansas Game and Fish Commission (AGFC) in 2024, changes the firearm options available to hunters across the eastern part of the state. It is not a statewide requirement the way some Midwest states once had—it's an added option in zones that were previously restricted to shotguns and muzzleloaders.

## Quick Answers: Arkansas Straight-Wall Cartridge Rules

**Is a straight-wall cartridge required to hunt deer in Arkansas?**
No. Unlike some Midwest states, Arkansas has never required straight-wall cartridges statewide. Zones 4 and 5 were simply restricted to shotguns and muzzleloaders during modern gun season—the 2024 rule change added non-semi-automatic straight-wall rifles as a new legal option in those zones, alongside the existing choices.

**What's the minimum caliber for a legal straight-wall cartridge in Arkansas?**
.30 caliber or larger, with no shoulder in the case. This is a lower floor than Michigan or Iowa, which sit around .35"–.357".

**Is the .350 Legend legal in Arkansas Zones 4 and 5?**
Yes. At .355" bullet diameter, it clears the .30 caliber floor in a non-semi-automatic action.

**Is the .450 Bushmaster legal in Arkansas Zones 4 and 5?**
Yes, in a non-semi-automatic rifle. Its .452" bullet diameter is well above the .30 caliber minimum.

**Can I use a semi-automatic rifle like an AR-15 platform?**
No. The Zone 4/5 modern gun season rule and the statewide Alternative Firearms Season both require a **non-semi-automatic** action—bolt, lever, pump, or single-shot only.

**Does this rule apply anywhere else in Arkansas?**
Yes, in a different form. The same class of cartridge (.30 caliber or larger, non-semi-auto) is legal statewide during the Alternative Firearms Season (formerly muzzleloader season). The Zone 4/5 modern-gun-season allowance is what's new and specific to those two zones.

## Why This Matters: A Different Kind of Flat Country

Most of our coverage of straight-wall cartridge (SWC) regulations focuses on Midwest row-crop country—Ohio, Michigan, Indiana, and Iowa. Arkansas earns a spot in this conversation too, but with an important caveat: SWC rules apply differently depending on where in the state you hunt, and the geography isn't uniform—not even within Zone 4 itself.

Zone 5 (the northern portion of Crowley's Ridge) and Zone 4's eastern section sit in the Arkansas Delta near the St. Francis and Mississippi Rivers—flat, soybean-and-rice country that looks and hunts a lot like the Midwestern row-crop ground we cover elsewhere on this site. Zone 4 also includes a separate, non-adjoining western section running along the Current, Black, and White River corridor near the Missouri border—a different landscape that doesn't fit the same row-crop mold. We're covering all of Zone 4 here for regulatory completeness, but our "corn-fed" framing really describes the Delta portions, not the river-corridor section.

Head further west into the Ozarks, and you're in a different world again—hill country where standard centerfire rifles have long been legal, with no SWC restriction to speak of.

## The Regulation, Precisely

For modern gun season in Zones 4 and 5, the requirements are:

- **The action:** Rifles must be **non-semi-automatic** (bolt-action, lever-action, pump, or single-shot).
- **The case profile:** The cartridge must be straight-walled, defined by AGFC as having **no shoulder** in the case. Extractor grooves or rims near the base are fine—AGFC's Enforcement Division has specifically clarified this distinction.
- **The caliber floor:** .30 caliber or larger.

> **Statewide context:** This article focuses on modern gun season in Zones 4 and 5, but Arkansas also allows this same class of straight-wall cartridge (.30 caliber or larger, non-semi-auto) statewide during the **Alternative Firearms Season**—a several-week fall season formerly known as muzzleloader season.

## The .30 Caliber Floor: A Different Baseline Than the Midwest

If you're used to Midwestern SWC rules, your mind probably jumps to cartridges like the .350 Legend, .400 Legend, or .450 Bushmaster. States like Michigan and Iowa enforce a caliber floor around .35"–.357".

Arkansas sets the bar lower, at **.30 caliber**. That opens the door to mild-shooting lever guns chambered in classic revolver cartridges like .357 Magnum or .44 Magnum, along with other straight-walled .30-caliber options that wouldn't qualify farther north.
